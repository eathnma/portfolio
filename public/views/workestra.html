Workestra

Wörkestra is a desktop and mobile app that allows remote coworkers to see and hear each other’s keyboard and mouse clicks. Wörkestra is an exploration of how teams can be more present with each other over distance while bringing joy into the remote workday.

Context

Our initial proposal for Workestra was to do a deep dive within the realm of remote-work investigating the space of remote work. Although conversations have been able to move online, remote teams have felt less connected than before.

Frictions

Through 60 survey respondants and 9 interviews, primarily from remote knowledge workers, we found three key insights. In order to created informed design, we created a 16 page research report that summarizing the context of remote work.

Duration

8 Weeks

Collaborators

Audrey Chow, Brendan Chow, Eliza Lim

Project Role

Interaction Design, Prototyping, User Research
I co-led the interaction design, but brought to life the interaction with a mix of prototyping tools, After Effects, Premiere Pro, and Audacity. User Research was conducted by the whole team, but I specifically focused on researching and understanding the importance of informal conversation in the workplace. I pulled key insights from both interviews and the literature review that ended up informing the final design decisions.
